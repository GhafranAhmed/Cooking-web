Ingredients:

The secret to a stellar chow mein lies in the quality of your ingredients.

Here’s what you’ll need:

Noodles:

  • Egg noodles or lo mein noodles are ideal.
  • Cook them al dente according to package directions.
  • Rinse with cold water to stop cooking and prevent sticking.

Protein:

  • Chicken breast or thigh, cut into bite-sized pieces.
  • You can substitute with shrimp, beef, or tofu.

Vegetables:

  • Carrots, julienned for a touch of sweetness and crunch.
  • Bell peppers, sliced for vibrant color and flavor.
  • Onions, sliced for a savory base.
  • Cabbage, shredded for volume and texture.
  • Snap peas or broccoli, optional, for added greens.

Chow Mein Sauce:

  • Soy sauce (low sodium), for that classic umami punch.
  • Oyster sauce (or vegetarian mushroom sauce), for depth and richness.
  • Sesame oil, for a nutty aroma.
  • Fresh ginger, minced for a zingy kick.
  • Garlic, minced for aromatic goodness.
  • Cornstarch, for thickening the sauce to perfection.
  • Chicken broth or water, to create the perfect sauce consistency.

Optional Garnishes:

  • Sesame seeds, for a nutty crunch.
  • Green onions, chopped for a fresh finish.

Step-by-Step: Chow mein!

Let’s get cooking!

Prep Work (2 minutes):

  • Chop all your vegetables and chicken.
  • Whisk together the chow mein sauce ingredients in a bowl.

Cook the Chicken (3 minutes):

  • Heat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  • Add a drizzle of oil and sauté the chicken until cooked through.
  • Remove the chicken from the pan and set it aside.

Sauté Vegetables (3 minutes):

  • Add the vegetables to the same skillet.
  • Sauté until tender-crisp, about 3 minutes.

Add Noodles and Sauce (5 minutes):

  • Add the cooked noodles to the skillet.
  • Add the cooked chicken back to the pan.
  • Pour the chow mein sauce over the noodles and vegetables.
  • Toss everything together until evenly coated and heated through.

Serve and Garnish (2 minutes):

  • Serve immediately, garnished with sesame seeds and green onions.

Tips and Tricks for Perfect Chow Mein

Some expert tips:

Noodle Preparation:

  • Don’t overcook your noodles. They should be al dente.
  • Rinse them with cold water to stop the cooking process and prevent sticking.

Vegetable Crispness:

  • Sauté your vegetables quickly over high heat to maintain their crunch.

Sauce Consistency:

  • Adjust the amount of cornstarch to achieve your desired sauce thickness.

Protein Variations:

  • Feel free to substitute chicken with shrimp, beef, or tofu.

Flavor Enhancements:

  • Add a dash of chili flakes or a squeeze of lime juice for extra zing.

Make it Veggie:

  • Omit the meat, and add extra firm tofu, or add extra vegetables like mushrooms.

Chicken Chow Mein Variations

Spicy Chicken Chow Mein:

  • Add chili garlic sauce or sriracha to the sauce.

Garlic Lovers Chicken Chow Mein:

  • Double the amount of minced garlic in the sauce.

Deluxe Chicken Chow Mein:

  • Add mushrooms, water chestnuts, or baby corn for a gourmet touch.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q: Can I use different types of noodles for this Chow Mein recipe?

A: Absolutely!

While egg noodles or lo mein noodles are traditional, you can also use rice noodles, udon noodles, or even spaghetti in a pinch.

Just adjust the cooking time accordingly.

Q: Can I make this Chow Mein vegetarian or vegan?

A: Yes!

To make it vegetarian, simply omit the chicken and add extra vegetables or tofu.

For a vegan version, use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth, and ensure your oyster sauce substitute is vegan-friendly.

Q: How do I prevent my noodles from sticking together?

A: The key is to rinse the noodles with cold water immediately after cooking.

This stops the cooking process and removes excess starch, preventing them from clumping.

Q: How long can I store leftover Chow Mein?

A: Leftover Chow Mein can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.

Reheat it gently on the stovetop or in the microwave.

Easy Falafel Recipe: Step-by-Step Guide to Crispy Perfection

You know that amazing falafel you get at your favorite Middle Eastern place? Those crispy, flavorful balls of goodness? Guess what? You can totally make them at home. And it's way easier than you think!

Falafel is a super popular food from the Middle East. It's basically a ball of chickpeas and herbs that's crispy on the outside but soft and fluffy inside. And the cool thing is, you can use it in so many ways. Throw it in a pita, make a salad, or just eat it as a snack.

Why Make Falafel Yourself?

There are tons of reasons to make your own falafel:

Better Taste: Homemade falafel tastes way better than what you get at most restaurants. It's fresher, and you can really taste the good stuff.

You're in Control: When you make it yourself, you know exactly what's going in it. You can use the best ingredients and make it just how you like it.

It's Healthier: You can make your falafel healthier by baking it instead of frying it. You also get to choose what kind of oil to use.

Save Some Cash: Making falafel at home is way cheaper than ordering it all the time.

Get in Touch with Culture: If you love Arab food, making falafel is a cool way to connect with that culture.

Ingredients

To make awesome falafel, you'll need these things:

Chickpeas:

You gotta use dried chickpeas, not the canned stuff. Soak them overnight. This is super important for the right texture.

Fresh Herbs:

Cilantro (if you're not into cilantro, you can use parsley instead)

Parsley

Onion and Garlic:

These give your falafel that classic flavor.

Spices:

Cumin

Coriander

You can also add a pinch of cayenne if you like it spicy.

Leavening:

Baking soda or baking powder. This helps make the falafel light and fluffy.

Flour:

Just a little bit to help hold everything together.

Oil:

For frying. Or you can bake them if you want.

Let's Make Falafel: Step by Step

Here's how to make falafel that's crispy on the outside and soft on the inside:

Get the Chickpeas Ready:

If you're using dried chickpeas (and you should!), soak them overnight.

Then, rinse and drain them really well.

Mix It All Up:

Put the chickpeas, herbs, onion, garlic, and spices in a food processor. Pulse it all together until it's finely ground. You don't want it to be a smooth paste.

Shape the Falafel:

You can make them into balls or patties, whatever you like. Make sure they're not too big, or they might not cook evenly.

Cook the Falafel:

Frying: Heat up some oil in a pan. Make sure it's hot! Then, carefully drop the falafel in and cook until they're golden brown and crispy.

Baking: If you want to bake them, put them on a baking sheet and bake until they're cooked through.

Pro Tips for Perfect Falafel

Want your falafel to be perfect every time?

Here are some tips:

If It's Too Dry:

Add a little bit of water to the mix.

If It's Too Mushy:

Add a little bit of flour.

For the Best Texture:

Don't overmix the batter.

Make sure the oil is hot when you're frying.

Don't put too many falafel in the pan at once.

Want to Change It Up?

Here are some ways to make your falafel a little different:

Change the Herbs:

Try adding some mint for a fresh flavor.

Add Veggies:

You can mix in some grated carrots or zucchini.

What to Do with Falafel

Falafel is awesome in so many dishes:

Classic Pita:

Put the falafel in a pita with hummus, tahini sauce, veggies, and pickles.

Falafel Salad:

Throw some falafel on a salad for a healthy and tasty meal.

Mezze Platter:

Serve falafel with other Middle Eastern dishes like hummus, baba ghanoush, and pita bread.

Awesome Sauces:

Make a tahini sauce for dipping. You can also use other sauces like yogurt sauce or hot sauce.

Falafel: Your Questions Answered

Here are some common questions about falafel:

What is falafel?

It's a fried ball or patty made from ground chickpeas or fava beans, mixed with herbs and spices.

What are the main ingredients in falafel?

Chickpeas, herbs (like cilantro and parsley), onion, garlic, and spices.

Do you have to use dried chickpeas for falafel?

Yes, dried chickpeas are the way to go for the best texture.

How long do you need to soak dried chickpeas?

Usually, overnight, or at least 8 hours.

How do you get falafel crispy?

Make sure your oil is hot and don't overcrowd the pan when frying.

How long does it take to cook falafel?

Frying usually takes about 3-5 minutes per batch.

Can you bake falafel instead of frying?

Yep, you can bake them for a healthier option.

Can falafel be made ahead of time?

You can make the batter ahead of time, but it's best to cook the falafel fresh.

What do you serve with falafel?

Pita bread, hummus, tahini sauce, salads, and other Middle Eastern dishes.

Is falafel vegan?

Most falafel recipes are vegan!
